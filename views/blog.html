MEDIA BIAS IN THE MODERN WORLD

August 31st, 2014 | Share with or
by Michael


First of all, I would like to suggest considering this article not as a scientific paper, as I am not an expert, but as an entertaining piece that could possibly give you new ideas. I would call this just a casual way of explaining my thoughts.

It is a common thing for us, human species, to put anything under perspective, to recreate facts and skew them in one direction or another. Most of us do it subconsciously, as our minds are not identical and our ways of understanding events are different. We had different experiences in life. In addition to that, do not forget social conditioning that each of us has gone through until this very moment. A combination of all those things creates this unique “lens”, through which we look at the world and “deform” a fact so it could fit under our reality. I consider this a “non-intentional” bias that in fact makes every story more relatable to others and not as robotic as it could be otherwise. This is a fascinating process, however this is not the main point of this article.

On the other hand, when there is intent in distorting facts, this is a totally different matter. When facts are skewed with purpose of giving a person imposed views on different social or politic issues, this become a real problem. The examples of this are unfortunately easy to find. Think of the last time you turned CNN. It has an obvious liberal “lens” and all reports and articles seem specifically tailored to the CNN’s blueprint, not regarding to what journalist was covering the story.

Unfortunately, this is a case for most major mainstream media. I am not even talking about “artificially created facts” that sometimes could be encountered on the most reputable sources of information. This is the case for Gaza-Israel, Ukraine-Russia, Syria conflicts and others that have been happening in the current world. I could say that media is an ultimate tool of the new century.

To conclude this, I would like to answer the question that you all probably have been asking yourselves. Yes, I am biased and so are you.
